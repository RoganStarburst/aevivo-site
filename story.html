The Aevivo Story

Built for what we couldn't find.

Peptides are everywhere. Rigor is rare. We started Aevivo to close that gap, one compound at a time.

Peptides had become one of the most talked-about categories in longevity. The supply chain behind them had become one of the least trustworthy. Origins were vague. Documentation was inconsistent. Buyers were asked to trust labels that no one could verify, paying premium prices for material that in many cases was sourced from wholesale suppliers in China, Pakistan, or India, repackaged, and marketed as European research-grade.

Aevivo was founded to build the supply we wanted to buy from. European synthesis. Verified purity. A Certificate of Analysis on record for every compound. Delivered in a sealed pen cartridge that protects the peptide from production to use. Named human beings behind the supply, available to answer questions and stand behind every batch.

How we met

Aevivo came out of a single evening. Suzanna and Tiago met at a friend's art exhibition, brought together by Caroline in a room of interesting people. They discovered they shared an almost identical obsession with longevity science. The conversation never really stopped, and somewhere in it the logical conclusion became that they should build something together rather than keep talking about it.

Where we're going

We are starting with a peptide catalog because it is the clearest entry point. The bigger work we are building toward is a full health journey: diagnostics, protocol design, and ongoing support from doctors, nurses, and coaches. Peptides will be one tool among many. The goal is to put the person at the centre of their own health, with a team behind them that actually shows up.

Together we're way more powerful than on our own. That is how this started. Suzanna Howse, Co-Founder

Co-Founder

Suzanna Howse.

Four decades of building businesses, now pointed at longevity.

Background

Forty years of travelling the globe and building businesses across multiple industries taught Suzanna something that sits at the core of Aevivo's brand: it is not what you have financially in life that matters most, it is the quality of life you lead. Her career has moved through entrepreneurship and operational leadership in more sectors than fit in a short page, always oriented around people, relationships, and standards of service.

Why peptides, why now

Suzanna's sphere of friends includes longevity-focused entrepreneurs and forward-thinkers who have been active in this space for more than five years. Two years ago she visited them in Colombia and had her first experience with an IV protocol. That session was the moment it clicked. Since then she has been studying the science, the protocols, and the market, with the intention of investing her capital, her knowledge, and her know-how into making longevity accessible to people who take their health seriously.

This is going to be my lifetime's work. Suzanna Howse

What she brings

Decades of business experience across industries. A deep network across longevity and entrepreneurship. An ability to read people, pivot, and operate through uncertainty. Strong personal standards for service and quality that she applies to every product and interaction Aevivo touches.

The frustration

What Suzanna sees in the current peptide market is not anger so much as impatience with misinformation. People have the willingness and the desire to improve their health, but they are self-medicating without the education or resources they need to do it safely. Her response at Aevivo is two-sided. First, build a properly regulated operation in Portugal and Europe as peptides move into the mainstream. Second, pair that supply with genuine education so buyers do not harm themselves.

Thirty-second bio

Tiago spent eleven years in a career that paid well and taught him a lot, then walked away to build something he actually believed in. He is co-founding Aevivo Longevity, a European research peptide company bringing GMP-certified compounds to a market that has been desperately waiting for a supplier it can actually trust. Right now he spends most of his time making sure that when Aevivo launches, the quality of what it puts out is impossible to argue with.

Why peptides, why now

The honest answer is that he spent a year after leaving his job searching for something that genuinely excited him, and the longevity space was the first thing he found where the more he learned, the more he wanted to know. The supply chain serving this science in Europe is broken in ways that are almost embarrassing, and he kept thinking someone should fix it. At some point he stopped waiting for that someone to appear.

I made the harder choice before Aevivo existed. I am not building this to escape something. I am building it because it is the only thing that makes sense right now. Tiago Areias

What he brings

What Tiago brings that the others do not is the willingness to have started. Leaving years of stability to build something uncertain is not a skill you can list on a CV, but it is the quality that makes everything else possible. He made the harder choice before Aevivo existed, which means he is not building this to escape something. He is building it because it is the only thing that makes sense right now.

The frustration

The peptide supply market in Europe is full of companies that expect buyers to simply trust them, with no documentation, no traceability, no real accountability for what is inside the vial. People are injecting compounds sourced from unverified manufacturers with no transparency on purity, origin, or production standards. The science behind these compounds is serious. The supply chain serving that science is not. That gap makes Tiago genuinely impatient.
