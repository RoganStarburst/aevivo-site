Privacy Policy.

Effective: May 2026 · Version 1.0 · Governing regulation: EU GDPR (Regulation 2016/679)

This Privacy Policy explains how Aevivo Longevity collects, uses, stores, and protects your personal data. We handle your information in full compliance with the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and Portuguese data protection law.

1. Who we are & data controller

The data controller responsible for your personal data is:

GÉNESE SOBRANCEIRA, LDA, doing business as Aevivo Longevity. NIF/NIPC: 519331800. INFARMED Cert. N.º 3800/DM/2026. Registered office: Calçada da Ajuda 35, Bloco 5, r/c esq., 1300-006 Lisboa, Portugal. Contact: info@aevivolongevity.com.

2. Personal data we collect

2.1 Contact & identity data

  • Full name
  • Email address
  • Phone number (if provided via WhatsApp or call booking)
  • Country of residence or business address

2.2 Order & transaction data

  • Peptides requested and quantities
  • Order reference numbers and correspondence
  • Billing and delivery address
  • Payment confirmation details (we do not store full card numbers)

2.3 Practitioner application data

  • Professional credentials, license number, or institutional affiliation
  • Clinic name, specialty, country of practice
  • Estimated monthly volume and peptides of interest

This category is collected to verify that purchases comply with our For Research Use Only (RUO) terms and to assess clinic supply fit. It is treated with a higher level of care and is not used for marketing purposes.

2.4 Communication data

  • Messages sent via our contact form, WhatsApp, or email
  • Records of call bookings and notes from consultations (where applicable)
  • Enquiry history

2.5 Website usage data

  • IP address, browser type, and device information
  • Pages visited and time spent on site (via analytics, where enabled)
  • Cookie data (see Section 10)

3. How we collect your data

  • Order forms submitted via our website
  • Practitioner application forms
  • Quote requests and audit requests
  • WhatsApp messages sent to our business line
  • Email correspondence
  • Website analytics tools (where enabled)

4. Legal basis for processing

  • Contract performance (Art. 6(1)(b)): to process and fulfil your orders, manage returns, and communicate about your purchases.
  • Legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f)): to verify buyer qualification, maintain records for regulatory compliance, prevent fraud, and protect the integrity of our RUO supply chain.
  • Legal obligation (Art. 6(1)(c)): to comply with applicable Portuguese and EU law, including record-keeping requirements.
  • Consent (Art. 6(1)(a)): where you have opted in to receive communications from us beyond what is necessary for order fulfilment. You may withdraw consent at any time.

5. How we use your data

  • Processing and fulfilling your orders, including cold-chain logistics coordination
  • Verifying practitioner credentials via the application form and maintaining compliance records
  • Communicating about orders, enquiries, and relevant product updates
  • Scheduling and conducting consultation calls
  • Responding to WhatsApp and email enquiries
  • Maintaining transaction records for accounting and legal compliance
  • Detecting and preventing fraud or misuse of our platform
  • Improving our website and service offering (using anonymised analytics where applicable)

We do not use your data for automated decision-making or profiling.

6. Data retention

  • Order and transaction data: retained for seven (7) years to meet Portuguese accounting and tax law requirements.
  • Application and credential data: retained for the duration of the business relationship plus five (5) years, to demonstrate compliance with our RUO obligations.
  • Communication data (email, WhatsApp): retained for three (3) years from last contact.
  • Website usage data: retained in accordance with our analytics provider's retention settings, typically 14 to 26 months.

When data is no longer required, it is securely deleted or anonymised.

7. Who we share your data with

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal data. We may share data with the following categories of third parties, strictly on a need-to-know basis:

  • Logistics and courier partners: name, delivery address, and order details necessary to fulfil cold-chain shipments.
  • Payment processors: transaction data required to process payments securely.
  • IT and communications providers: hosting, email, and scheduling tools used to operate our business.
  • Legal and regulatory authorities: where required by law or to respond to a lawful request from a competent authority.

8. International data transfers

Where any of our service providers are based outside the European Economic Area (EEA), we ensure that appropriate safeguards are in place, including Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs), transfers to countries with an EU adequacy decision, or other lawful transfer mechanisms permitted by the GDPR.

9. Your rights under GDPR

  • Right of access (Art. 15)
  • Right to rectification (Art. 16)
  • Right to erasure (Art. 17)
  • Right to restriction of processing (Art. 18)
  • Right to data portability (Art. 20)
  • Right to object (Art. 21)
  • Right to withdraw consent

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at sh@aevivolongevity.com. We respond within one calendar month. If you are not satisfied with our response, you may lodge a complaint with the Portuguese data protection authority, Comissão Nacional de Proteção de Dados (CNPD), cnpd.pt.

10. Cookies

Our website may use cookies and similar tracking technologies. Essential cookies are required for the website to function correctly. Analytics cookies are only placed with your consent. You can manage cookie preferences through your browser settings.

11. Data security

We take reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data, including secure encrypted transmission via HTTPS, internal access controls, and use of reputable, GDPR-compliant service providers. In the event of a personal data breach likely to result in a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify you and the CNPD as required.

12. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The current version will always be published with its effective date. Where changes are material, we will notify active customers by email.
