This Privacy Policy explains how AEVIVO Longevity collects, uses, stores, and protects your personal data. We are committed to handling your information responsibly and in full compliance with the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and Portuguese data protection law.

The data controller responsible for your personal data is:

GENESE SOBRANCEIRA, LDA

Registered in: Portugal

2. Personal Data We Collect

We collect personal data in the following categories:

2.1 Contact & Identity Data

  • Full name
  • Email address
  • Phone number (if provided via WhatsApp or call booking)
  • Country of residence or business address

2.2 Order & Transaction Data

  • Peptides requested and quantities
  • Order reference numbers and correspondence
  • Billing and delivery address
  • Payment confirmation details (we do not store full card numbers)

2.3 Research Protocol Form Data

  • Professional credentials or institutional affiliation (where provided)
  • Research context and intended use of peptides
  • Buyer qualification information submitted via our protocol form

This category of data is collected to verify that purchases comply with our For Research Use Only (RUO) terms. It is treated with a higher level of care and is not used for marketing purposes.

2.4 Communication Data

  • Messages sent via our contact form, WhatsApp, or email
  • Records of call bookings and notes from consultations (where applicable)
  • Enquiry history

2.5 Website Usage Data

  • IP address, browser type, and device information
  • Pages visited and time spent on site (via analytics, where enabled)
  • Cookie data (see Section 10)

3. How We Collect Your Data

We collect personal data through the following channels:

  • Order forms submitted via our website (aevivolongevity.com)
  • Research protocol forms completed as part of the buyer qualification process
  • Call bookings made via our website scheduler
  • WhatsApp messages sent to our business line
  • Email correspondence
  • Website analytics tools (if and where enabled)

4. Legal Basis for Processing

We process your personal data on the following legal bases under Article 6 of the GDPR:

  • Contract performance (Art. 6(1)(b)): to process and fulfil your orders, manage returns, and communicate about your purchases.
  • Legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f)): to verify buyer qualification, maintain records for regulatory compliance, prevent fraud, and protect the integrity of our RUO supply chain.
  • Legal obligation (Art. 6(1)(c)): to comply with applicable Portuguese and EU law, including record-keeping requirements.
  • Consent (Art. 6(1)(a)): where you have opted in to receive communications from us beyond what is necessary for order fulfilment. You may withdraw consent at any time.

5. How We Use Your Data

We use your personal data for the following purposes:

  • Processing and fulfilling your orders, including cold-chain logistics coordination
  • Verifying buyer qualification via the protocol form and maintaining compliance records
  • Communicating with you about your orders, enquiries, and any relevant product updates
  • Scheduling and conducting consultation calls
  • Responding to WhatsApp and email enquiries
  • Maintaining transaction records for accounting and legal compliance
  • Detecting and preventing fraud or misuse of our platform
  • Improving our website and service offering (using anonymised analytics where applicable)

We do not use your data for automated decision-making or profiling.

6. Data Retention

We retain your personal data for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected, subject to the following:

  • Order and transaction data: retained for seven (7) years to meet Portuguese accounting and tax law requirements.
  • Protocol form and buyer qualification data: retained for the duration of the business relationship plus five (5) years, to demonstrate compliance with our RUO obligations.
  • Communication data (email, WhatsApp): retained for three (3) years from last contact.
  • Website usage data: retained in accordance with our analytics provider's retention settings, typically 14 to 26 months.

When data is no longer required, it is securely deleted or anonymised.

7. Who We Share Your Data With

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal data. We may share data with the following categories of third parties, strictly on a need-to-know basis:

  • Logistics and courier partners: name, delivery address, and order details necessary to fulfil cold-chain shipments.
  • Payment processors: transaction data required to process payments securely. Payment processors operate under their own GDPR-compliant privacy policies.
  • IT and communications providers: hosting, email, and scheduling tools used to operate our business (e.g., website hosting, WhatsApp Business).
  • Legal and regulatory authorities: where required by law or to respond to a lawful request from a competent authority.

All third parties with whom we share data are required to process it lawfully and in accordance with applicable data protection law.

8. International Data Transfers

Where any of our service providers are based outside the European Economic Area (EEA), we ensure that appropriate safeguards are in place for the transfer of your data, including:

  • Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) approved by the European Commission;
  • Transfers to countries with an EU adequacy decision; or
  • Other lawful transfer mechanisms as permitted by the GDPR.

You may request further information about the safeguards in place for any specific transfer by contacting us at sh@aevivolongevity.com.

9. Your Rights Under GDPR

As a data subject under the GDPR, you have the following rights:

  • Right of access (Art. 15): to request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to rectification (Art. 16): to request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Right to erasure (Art. 17): to request deletion of your data, subject to our legal retention obligations.
  • Right to restriction of processing (Art. 18): to request that we limit how we use your data in certain circumstances.
  • Right to data portability (Art. 20): to receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Right to object (Art. 21): to object to processing based on legitimate interests.
  • Right to withdraw consent: where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw it at any time without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at sh@aevivolongevity.com. We will respond within one calendar month. If you are not satisfied with our response, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the Portuguese data protection authority:

Comissão Nacional de Proteção de Dados (CNPD)
Website: www.cnpd.pt · Phone: +351 213 928 400

10. Cookies

Our website may use cookies and similar tracking technologies for the following purposes:

  • Essential cookies: required for the website to function correctly (e.g., session management). These cannot be disabled.
  • Analytics cookies: used to understand how visitors interact with our site (e.g., pages visited, time on site). These are only placed with your consent.

You can manage cookie preferences through your browser settings or via any cookie consent tool displayed on our website. Disabling analytics cookies will not affect your ability to place orders or use our services.

11. Data Security

We take reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data against unauthorised access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction. These include:

  • Secure encrypted transmission of data via HTTPS
  • Access controls limiting who within our team can access personal data
  • Use of reputable, GDPR-compliant service providers for hosting and communications

In the event of a personal data breach that is likely to result in a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify you and the CNPD in accordance with our obligations under Articles 33 and 34 of the GDPR.
